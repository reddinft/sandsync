## Andrej Karpathy — `autoresearch`

**Repo:** https://github.com/karpathy/autoresearch  
**Author:** Andrej Karpathy  

The SandSync agent pipeline's quality gate design — where Ogma (LLM-as-judge) scores Anansi's chapter drafts and sends them back for revision if they fall below a quality threshold — is directly inspired by the **generate → evaluate → keep/discard → iterate** inner loop pattern from `autoresearch`.

Karpathy's key insight: an AI research system should never stop iterating toward quality, with evaluation as a first-class citizen of the loop rather than an afterthought. We applied this to storytelling: no chapter reaches the reader until it has passed Ogma's quality gate (score ≥ 7.5/10), with up to 2 automatic revision cycles before force-approval.

The `program.md` pattern — specifying agent methodology as a structured document rather than code — also influenced our agent charter design for the broader Redditech Labs agent team.

## Caribbean Folklore Tradition

The stories generated by SandSync draw on a living oral tradition. We acknowledge:

- **Anansi stories** — West African (Ashanti) and Caribbean trickster spider mythology, brought to the Caribbean through the African diaspora
- **Papa Bois** — Trinidadian forest guardian spirit (also *Maître Bois*)
- **La Diablesse, Soucouyant, Lagahoo, Douen** — Trinidadian and wider Caribbean folklore figures

These are not invented for this project. They belong to the communities that kept them alive. We use them with respect and with the intent to share, not appropriate.
